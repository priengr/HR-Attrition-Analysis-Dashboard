# 📊 HR Analytics Dashboard — Employee Attrition Analysis

![Dashboard Preview](Screenshots/dashboard_overview.png)

## 📌 Project Overview

This project presents an interactive HR Analytics Dashboard built in Power BI
to analyze employee attrition across a 1,470-employee organization.
The dashboard provides HR teams with data-driven insights to identify
attrition patterns and design targeted retention strategies.

---

## 🔴 Business Problem

The HR department lacked visibility into key workforce metrics:

- No clear view of total employee count or active vs. inactive employees
- No standardized method to track attrition count or calculate attrition rate
- Unable to identify which departments, age groups, or education fields
  had the highest attrition
- No job satisfaction tracking to predict future attrition risk
- Gender and marital status attrition patterns were completely unknown

## 📊 Dashboard Overview

### KPI Cards
- Employee Count: 1,470
- Attrition Count: 237
- Attrition Rate: 16.1%
- Active Employees: 1,233
- Average Age: 37

### Charts & Visualizations
- Attrition by Gender (Pie Chart)
- Department-Wise Attrition (Pie Chart)
- Number of Employees by Age Group (Bar Chart)
- Education Field-Wise Attrition (Bar Chart)
- Attrition by Marital Status (Bar Chart)
- Job Satisfaction Rating by Job Role (Matrix Table)

---

## 💡 Key Insights

- 📌 Attrition rate of **16.1%** exceeds the 10–15% industry benchmark
- 📌 **Sales department** has the highest attrition at 65.37% of total exits
- 📌 **60% of exits are Male** (882) vs 40% Female (588)
- 📌 **Age group 25–34** is the largest workforce segment (554 employees)
- 📌 **Life Sciences** education field has the highest attrition (89 employees)
- 📌 **Single employees** have the highest attrition count (120)
- 📌 **85 Sales Executives** rated job satisfaction at Level 1 (lowest)

---

## 🚀 Business Impact

Based on these insights, the organization can:

- Prioritize retention programs in the **Sales department** immediately
- Design **gender-specific** engagement initiatives
- Create career growth paths for the **25–34 age group**
- Launch wellbeing programs for **single employees**
- Use Job Satisfaction ratings as an **early attrition warning system**
